Materials for tailoring hakama

Fabric, which in ancient Japanese wrapped around the hips and called - hakama. It is used for its production of silk.

It is suitable for the manufacture of hakama because of dense tissue that keeps its shape well: It is based on cotton fabric, flax, with added synthetics. Color can be any.

Traditional color hakama for aikido is black, rarely white.

Today hakama are made of high quality durable fabrics:

    Greta (210 g / m2, 55% cotton, 45% synthetic);

    Twill (210 g / m2, 35% cotton, 65% synthetic);

    Orton (270 g / m2, 55% cotton, 45% synthetic).

Standard color is black. There are blue, blue, red and green. Durable synthetic thread is in matching color.

>As practice shows, hakama, sewn from these tissues, distinguishes strength, durability, and easy care.
