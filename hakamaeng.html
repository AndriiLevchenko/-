Hakama - the type of traditional Japanese clothing.

Originally it was worn only by men. But today it is also worn by both women and man.

There are several types of hakama. Hakama for aikido is long pants, pleated like a skirt or trousers, fastened on the waist and falls to the ankle. Hakama is worn over a kimono.


It has 4 belts (Ximo): 2 long, located on the sides of the front and two short, spaced laterally behind.







The back of the dress has a rigid trapezoidal section, called koshiita, and is located below inside the element, in the form of a spoon, called Hera, which helps store the folded hakama.






Hakama has seven deep wrinkles: two on the rear panel and five on the front. Location of forward folds (three right, left two) are asymmetrically, but it looks balanced and is an example of asymmetry Japanese aesthetics.
