Measurements

In ancient Japan hakama was made of the same size for all, and then customized for each person specifically.

Today a wide range of sizes of any sportswear is available, but the acquisition of the hakama should be borne in mind that the discrepancy between the size of clothes an athlete wears may hamper his movements, increasing demands on the fit.

Here is where help comes in by professionals who accurately reproduce hakama in the desired length, width, etc., for a particular athlete.

H1 measurement is taken at the waist. If you have difficulty with this, please select a marker on the place where you would normally locate the belt (his middle height). W1 and W2 measurements are taken at the waist (at the location of the belt) and the widest part of your hips, respectively.

The length of the hakama will be to the ankle, usually 5 - 6 cm from the floor. To make it easier for beginners to do the exercises, it is sometimes advised to shorten the hakama (up to 7 cm from the floor).

In the present embodiment, sewing the hakama you order, removal of measurements is made directly to a specific athlete. Most accurately it is taken into account his anthropological features that ensure its comfort during exercise and improve athletic performance.
